Experience

Early Acting Experience

In 1967, he performed the opera "I Love the Red Maiden" on Broadway. It was Freeman's first regular theatrical performance. The following year, Harodori made him famous. After making a name on Broadway, Morgan Freeman moved to television. From 1971 to 1976, he performed Mr. Read Well on American Public Television Children's Programs for six years.

1967 - 1976

Initial Oscar Nomination

It wasn't until 1987 that Freeman really got up from The Black Prison. The pimp in the film won him two nominations for Best Supporting Actor at the Oscar and Best Supporting Actor at the Golden Globe Award. Three films in 1989 made Freeman lucky one after another. First, before dawn, Freeman played a loyal, honest, kind and brave old driver. His performance won him the Obi Award and nominated him for Best Actor of the Academy Award.

1987 - 1989

Further Development

In the 1990s, Morgan Freeman played a Moorish warrior in Robin Hood; in Unforgivable, he played a kind, honest, brave and strong old gunman in the West; in Seven Sins, he created the image of a sober, calm, witty and agile old detective; and in Shawshank's Redemption. The film also gave him his third Oscar nomination. In 2005, Morgan Freeman won the 77th Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor for Million Babies.

1990 - 2005

Up to now

In 2012, his Batman: The Rise of the Dark Knight was released. On April 18, 2014, Morgan Freeman's The Transcendental Hacker was released. On June 24, 2016, Morgan Freeman's crime action film "The Devil's Bandit 2" was released in China. Morgan Freeman starred in "Now You See Me 2" and "London Has Fallen".On August 24, 2017, he was awarded the Lifelong Achievement Award at the 24th American Actors'Union Award.

Oscar Awards

June 1, 1937
Born

Morgan Freeman was born in Memphis, Tennessee. Morgan Freeman first appeared on stage at the age of 8 and played the leading role in school drama. He won an interstate drama contest at the age of 12 and performed in a radio play in Nashville in high school. In 1955, Morgan Freeman refused part of Jackson State University's theatre scholarship and chose to become a mechanic in the U.S. Air Force. Freeman joined the Air Force at the age of 18 and retired gloriously in 1959.

Oblivion

"Oblivion" is a science fiction film released by Universal Pictures, directed by Joseph Kosinsky, starring Tom Cruise, Oga Corelanco, Andrea Respologg and Morgan Freeman. The film was released in the United States on April 19, 2013. The film tells the story of a soldier Jack who was assigned to a distant planet on a patrol mission. On the planet, he met a ferocious alien community and a beautiful woman who was stranded here because of the crash of a spaceship. The soldier began to question his world outlook in the course of his interaction with the woman.

  • Role Played: Malcolm Beech
  • Date: April 19, 2013
  • Director: Joseph Kosinski

The Bucket List

"The Bucket List" is a feature film directed by Robert Lenner and starring Jack Nicholson, Morgan Freeman, Sean Hayes and Beavery Todd. It was released in the United States on December 25, 2007. The film tells of two cancer patients who, by coincidence, met and became good friends. The two decided to complete the "wish list" story they had in mind for the rest of their lives.

  • Role Played: Carter Chambers
  • Date: December 25, 2007
  • Director: Robert Lenner

Now You See Me

"Now You See Me " is a magic crime film directed by Louis Lettler and starring Jesse Eisenberg, Woody Harrison, Ella Fischer, Dave Frank and Morgan Freeman. The film tells the story of a group of highly intelligent thieves who use the most sophisticated technology and the magnificent stage as a cover to complete the day-to-day theft in the eyes of the public. The film was released in North America on May 31, 2013.

The Shawshank Redemption

"Shawshank's Redemption" is directed by Frank Darabont and starred by Tim Robbins and Morgan Freeman. The film is adapted from the novel of the same name in Stephen King's Four Seasons, which covers the theme of "hope". Through the special background of prison, which is compulsory deprivation of liberty and highly emphasis on discipline, the film shows the fear of "time passing, environmental transformation" of individuals. The film ends with a revenge catharsis in the form of Count of Monte Cristo. In IMDB, more than 1.6 million members selected the first of 250 best movies. It was included in the list of the American Film Society's top 100 movies of the 20th century.

  • Role Player: Alice Pound Rhett
  • Date: September 23, 1994
  • Director: Frank Darabont
